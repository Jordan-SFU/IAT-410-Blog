We also received a bunch of good feedback during the review process, and compiled them into a short list:

  • Level ramping: make level 1 much easier.
  • Level ramping: limit panel destruction to certain panel types per level.
  • Level ramping: spread alien types out by level.
  • Level ramping: introduce more levels to space out new systems.
  • Combat tuning: halve alien counts, increase turret damage, and add more knockback.
  • Rewards: add Medbay support into progression planning.
  • Repair tuning: reduce asteroid damage.
  • Visuals: bullets should emit from the turret nozzle.

The hardest part this week was balancing level progression. We had a lot of good features, but making them arrive at the right pace was the key challenge.

A lot of the feedback pointed to pacing, not individual mechanics. Our game was much too difficult, and players were seeing too many systems too quickly, so our biggest priority was to spread pressure out over more levels instead of trying to introduce everything everywhere at once.

We also reviewed combat density and tuned around clarity. Reducing alien count helped make combat feel less noisy and more intentional, which helped players understand why they were succeeding or failing.

On the visuals side, the turret projectile origin change sounds minor, but anchoring bullets to the nozzle improves legibility and moment-to-moment polish in a big way.

Overall, Week 5 felt like the transition from feature growth to controlled tuning. We left the week with stronger enemy readability, better presentation in menus and dialogue, and a clearer roadmap for how the beta should ramp into the final build.
